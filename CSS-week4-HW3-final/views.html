View from My Window

A window is a frame you return to again and again throughout the day, and yet the view it holds is never quite the same twice. The buildings captured in these photographs stand in the same positions they always have, but the quality of the light, the presence of clouds, the season — all of these shift the scene constantly. What looks grey and heavy on an overcast morning can glow warmly by late afternoon. Photographing the same view multiple times reveals how much a fixed scene can change, and how much we miss when we only glance rather than look.

These six photographs are taken from a single window, facing the same direction each time, yet each image has its own mood and character. The buildings outside are not landmarks — they are simply the structures that make up a neighborhood, ordinary architecture doing its quiet job of housing lives and activities. But looked at closely, they offer surprising variety: different textures of wall, different arrangements of windows, different ways that shadows fall across surfaces. This is what any window offers, if you are willing to stand at it long enough to really see.
